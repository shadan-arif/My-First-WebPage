Know the terms

  • NATIONALISM:
    Strong identification of group of individuals with a political entity defined in national terms

  • SATYAGRAH:
    Appeal for truth. It is based on ideals of truth and non-violence

  • ROWLATT ACT:
    Act which gave the government enormous power to repress political.Allowed that govt. could arrest anybody without a trial for 2 years.

  • NON-COOPERATION MOVEMENT:
    Aim of this movement is not to cooperate with the British.

  • SWADESHI:
    Involved boycotting British products & the revival of domestic made products.

  • BOYCOOT:
    Form of consumers activism involving the act of voluntarily abstainig from using,buying or dealing with a person,for political reason.

  • PICKET:
    Form of demonstration or protest by which people block the entrance to a shop, factory or office

  • SWARAJ:
    Means freedom or self rule.

  • SIMON COMMISSION:
    New tory government in britain constituted a statutory Commission under Sir John Simon.It arrived in India in1928.

  • GANDHI IRWIN PACT:
    Mahatama Gandhi entered into a pact with Lord Erwin on 5th march 1931.Under this pact Gandhiji consented to participate in a Round Table Conference

  • FOLKLORES:
    Many leaders took help od folk tales to spread the idea of nationalism.It was believed that the folk tales revealed the true picture of traditional culture.
