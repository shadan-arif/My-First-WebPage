Differing Strands within the Movement

    The Movements In The Town

  • Started with middle-class participation.
  • Council elections were boycotted except in Madras.
  • Effects of non-cooperation on the economic front were dramatic:
    1. Foreign goods were boycotted.
    2. Liquor shops picketed.
    3. Foreign cloth burnt in huge bonfires.
  • As the boycott movement spread,people began wearing only Indian clothes, production of Indian textile mills and handlooms went up.
  • This movement in the cities gradually slowed down for a variety of reasons:
    1. Khadi cloth was often more expensive than mass produced mill cloth.
    2. The boycott of British institutions posed a problem.
    3. Students and Teachers began trickling back to government schools.
    4. Lawyers joined back work in government courts.


Rebellion in the Countryside

-Awadh Peasant Movement

  • Peasants were led by Baba Ramchandra.
  • Movement was against talukdars and landlords who demanded from peasants high rents.
  • Peasants had to do begar and work at landlords’ farms without any payment.
  • The peasant movement had following demands:
    1. Abolition of begar
    2. Reduction of revenue
    3. Social boycott of oppressive landlords
  • Jawaharlal Nehru began talking to the villagers trying to understand their grievances.
  • As the movement spread the houses of talukdars and merchants were attacked, bazaars were looted, and grain hoards were taken over.
  • In many places local leaders told peasants that Gandhiji had declared that no taxes were to be paid.


-Guerrilla Movement in Andhra Pradesh

  • In forest regions, the colonial government had closed forest areas, preventing people from entering the forests to graze their cattle, or to collect fuelwood and fruits.
  • People's livelihood was affected also their traditional rights were being denied.
  • Alluri Sitaram Raju claimed that he had a variety of special powers:
    1. He could make correct astrological predictions
    2. Heal people
    3. Survive bullet shots.
    4. Raju talked of the greatness of Mahatma Gandhi.
    5. He was inspired by the Non-Cooperation Movement.
    6. Persuaded people to wear khadi
    7. And give up drinking
    8. Asserted that India could be liberated only by the use of force.
  • Raju was captured by gudem rebels and executed in 1924, and over time became a folk hero.

-Swaraj In The Plantations

  • For plantation workers in Assam, freedom meant the right to move freely in and out.
  • Under the Inland Emigration Act of 1859, plantation workers were not permitted to leave the tea gardens without permission
  • When they heard of the Non-Cooperation Movement,they left the plantations and headed home.
  • They believed that by Gandhi Raj everyone would be given land .
  • Stranded on the way by a railway and steamer strike,they were brutally beaten up by police .
