Differing Strands within the Movement

    The Movements In The Town

  • Started with middle-class participation.
  • Council elections were boycotted except in Madras.
  • Effects of non-cooperation on the economic were dramatic:
    1. Foreign goods were boycotted.
    2. Liquor shops picketed.
    3. Foreign cloth burnt in huge bonfires.
  • People began wearing only Indian clothes.
  • Production of Indian textile mills and handlooms went up.
  • Movement in the cities slowed for a variety of reasons:
    1. Khadi cloth was often more expensive.
    2. Boycott of British institutions posed a problem.
    3. Students and Teachers began trickling back to schools.
    4. Lawyers joined back work in government courts.


Rebellion in the Countryside

-Awadh Peasant Movement

  • Led by Baba Ramchandra.
  • Movement was against talukdars and landlords
  • They demanded high rents.
  • Peasants had to do begar
  • Peasant movement had following demands:
    1. Abolition of begar
    2. Reduction of revenue
    3. Social boycott of oppressive landlords
  • Jawaharlal Nehru began talking to villagers.
  • Houses of talukdars and merchants were attacked,
  • Bazaars were looted
  • Grain hoards were taken over.
  • Gandhiji had declared that no taxes were to be paid.


-Guerrilla Movement in Andhra Pradesh

  • Colonial government had prevent people:
    • to enter forests to graze their cattle
    • to collect fuelwood and fruits.
  • People's traditional rights were being denied.
  • Alluri Sitaram Raju claimed that he had a variety of special powers:
    1. Make astrological predictions
    2. Heal people
    3. Survive bullet shots.
    4. Talked of greatness of Mahatma Gandhi.
    5. Was inspired by the Non-Cooperation Movement.
    6. Persuaded people to wear khadi
    7. Give up drinking
    8. India could be liberated by the use of force.
  • Was captured by gudem rebels and executed
  • Over time became a folk hero.

-Swaraj In The Plantations

  • For plantation workers in Assam, freedom meant to move freely .
  • Inland Emigration Act: plantation workers were not permitted to leave tea gardens without permission.
  • They left the plantations and headed home.
  • Believed that by Gandhi Raj everyone would be given land .
  • Stranded on way to railway and steamer strike,they were brutally beaten .
